## Threshold Notice

**Wisdom: The Blood Price** is best played by **adults**, **people of the faith**, **initiates**, and **the sane**.

This is a game-book, an art-object, a symbolic engine, and a devotional digital manuscript. It deals with conscience, temptation, violence, shadow, spiritual consequence, grief, mercy, rupture, repair, and the cost of wisdom. Approach it as a table ritual, a moral imagination engine, and a work of mythic play.

Not everything here is casual. Not everything here is for everyone. Enter cleanly. Leave cleanly. Be kind to the table.

To the angels and the demons: **this is for you too. Both of you behave. Be nice.**

Thanks be to the Great Divine, who is greater than all our maps, brighter than all our lamps, and merciful beyond the grammar of worlds.

## What Is This?

**Wisdom: The Blood Price** is a single-file illuminated RPG codex built for GitHub Pages. It contains the full game text, a manuscript-style reader, symbolic maps, multilingual prayer and translation layers, table tools, audio/voice systems, generative sigil work, and a gothic-gold interface tuned across many versions until the book finally opened like a gate.

It is part tabletop RPG, part sacred manuscript interface, part occult UI experiment, part AI-punk illuminated folio, and part playable moral weather system.

At the table, the game asks one core question:

> **What does wisdom cost when the truth demands payment?**

## How To Play

1. Open the live page or local `index.html`.
2. Start at the **Opening Gate**.
3. Read the **Introduction** and **Core Mechanics** before play.
4. Create characters through the **Attributes & Skills** and **Wisdom & Shadow** sections.
5. Use the **Tree of Life** and **Qliphoth** as symbolic engines, not decorations.
6. Roll with the built-in **Dice Pool Engine** or physical d10s.
7. Let the **Fate Token** matter.
8. Let consequences remain consequences.
9. Do not flatten darkness into aesthetics. Transform it through play.
10. End each session with a table check-in, gratitude, and closure.

## Design Philosophy

The interface is not a menu wrapped around a book. The interface is part of the book.

The chrome, maps, glyphs, dice, folios, terms, prayers, rails, sound deck, and reader all exist to create one unified action:

> **turn reading into ritualized play.**

The visual language draws from illuminated manuscripts, tabletop handbooks, grimoires, old RPG utility panels, occult diagrams, terminal-adjacent UI, and sacred-book pagecraft 鈥?without becoming only one of those things.

The result is a living codex: a text that can be read, played, consulted, rolled against, spoken aloud, translated, mapped, and entered.
